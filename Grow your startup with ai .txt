1. The "Singular Accent" Color Palette
Do not try to match or complement this green with other bright colors; it will instantly cheapen the look.
* The Foundation: Stick strictly to deep blacks, stark whites, and cool slate grays.
* The Accent: Use this exact lime green as your absolute, uncompromising primary action color. Every hover state, active CTA button, and vital data point should use this green. Against a dark, minimalist background, this color will practically glow, looking expensive and highly intentional.
2. Typography: The Rule of Contrast
The organic, handwritten nature of the logo demands a hyper-structured counterpart in the text.
* Continue using a massive, unapologetic neo-grotesque sans-serif (like Inter or Helvetica Now) for your massive headlines.
* The friction between your sharp, rigid grid typography and the fluid, human stroke of the logo is a classic hallmark of high-end, disruptive brand design. It visually represents the collision of human creativity and AI precision.
3. Texture and Cinematic Depth
Since the logo has a painted, brush-stroke quality, you can introduce subtle, sophisticated textures into the strict grid to bridge the gap between "technical" and "organic."
* In the dark-mode "Problem" section, introduce deep, monochromatic ink washes or fluid, slow-moving watercolor-like textures deep in the background. Keep them almost entirely black or dark gray, visible only through cinematic studio lighting effects. This adds a luxurious, tactile feel that echoes the logo's stroke without breaking the minimalist vibe.
4. UI and Animation Updates
* Laser-Sharp Interactions: When a user hovers over a dark, structural element (like a speaker card or an output icon), have the border or a subtle glowing shadow snap to that lime green.
* Cursor Dynamics: Consider a custom cursor—a stark white or lime green geometric shape (like a simple dot or crosshair) that inverses colors when it hovers over different sections.
